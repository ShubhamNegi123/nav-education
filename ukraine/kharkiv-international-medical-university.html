Kharkiv International Medical
University

Kharkiv International Medical University (KhIMU) has secured its place in the top-10 private universities in Ukraine. Students can choose KhIMU for MBBS in Ukraine as it is the first international medical university in Ukraine and of the European level.
KhIMU was established in 2018 and it has already matched the European quality standards that provide students opportunities for their career as well as self-improvement. Here students get to learn their medical courses from the brilliant professors and scientists of their particular fields.
There are scholarship plans for Indian students in KhIMU where they offer scholarships up to 50% of the tuition fees. Students can apply online anytime as the online application process is active all year, yet there are only 2 particular timelines when they accept admissions i.e. twice a year.

ADVANTAGES TO STUDY IN UKRAINE FOR INDIAN STUDENTS

  • Kharkiv International Medical University is the first international medical university in Ukraine.
  • It is licensed as a self-financed private institute by the Ministry of Education and Science of Ukraine.
  • KhIMU maintains an international environment where the lecturers, as well as students, are from all over the world.
  • Students get an excellent teaching experience and good quality education for a very affordable fee.
  • Students get to practice on the medical equipment that is built by modern technologies and useful for better understanding in studies.
  • At KhIMU students get to attend international conferences and workshops with brilliant scientists and doctors.
  • Modern and well-equipped lecture halls, laboratories, and libraries with all the required books are available for students.
  • The University has taken care of the accommodation of students with all the necessary amenities and sports, gym, canteen, available on the campus.
  • KhIMU has special advantages and facilities to offer to Indian students.
  • There is a special scholarship of up to 50% of the tuition fee for Indian students.
